Hong Kong Style Mahjong

Overview

Mahjong is a tile-based game that originated in China during the Qing dynasty. The name mahjong (麻雀) translates to "sparrow" — evoking the chattering sound tiles make as they're shuffled across the table. Here at the Sparrow, we play by Hong Kong Old Style rules, the most widely played variant worldwide.

Four players sit at the table. The goal is to be the first to assemble a complete hand of 4 sets plus 1 pair (14 tiles total) by drawing and discarding tiles.

Gameplay

Setup

  1. All 144 tiles are shuffled and arranged into a wall.
  2. The dealer (East wind) is determined. Play proceeds counter-clockwise: East → South → West → North.
  3. Each player is dealt 13 tiles. The dealer receives 14 tiles and takes the first turn by discarding.

Turn Structure

  1. Draw — Take one tile from the wall.
  2. Declare (optional) — Declare a concealed Kong or win (Mahjong).
  3. Discard — Place one tile face-up in the discard pool.

Claiming Discards

After a player discards, other players may claim the discarded tile, in this priority order:

  1. Win (Mahjong / 糊) — Any player who can complete their hand. Highest priority.
  2. Kong (槓) or Pong (碰) — Any player with 3 or 2 matching tiles.
  3. Chow (上) — Only the next player in turn order, to form a sequence.

If multiple players claim, the higher-priority claim wins. If no one claims, the next player draws from the wall.

Tip: Claiming a Pong or Chow exposes those tiles face-up on the table. This reveals information to your opponents and may prevent you from earning bonus faan for a concealed hand. Claim strategically!

Sets & Winning

Types of Sets

  • Chow (上) — Three consecutive tiles in the same suit (e.g., 2-3-4 of Bamboo). Cannot use honor tiles.
  • Pong (碰) — Three identical tiles (e.g., three West wind tiles).
  • Kong (槓) — Four identical tiles. Declared explicitly and replaced with a wall draw.
  • Pair (眼/將) — Two identical tiles. Every winning hand needs exactly one pair.

Standard Winning Hand

A standard winning hand consists of 4 sets + 1 pair = 14 tiles. Sets can be any combination of Chows, Pongs, and Kongs. You need exactly one pair.

Ways to Win

  • Self-Drawn (自摸) — Complete your hand by drawing from the wall. Awards 1 bonus faan.
  • Discard Win — Complete your hand using another player's discard.

Special Hands

  • Thirteen Orphans (十三幺) — One of each: 1 & 9 of all three suits, all four winds, all three dragons, plus one duplicate. A limit hand (13 faan).
  • Seven Pairs (七對子) — Seven distinct pairs. Worth 4 faan.

Kong Rules

There are three types of Kong:

  • Exposed Kong — Claim a discard when you hold three matching tiles. All four tiles are shown.
  • Concealed Kong — Declare during your turn when you hold four matching tiles in your hand.
  • Added Kong — Add the fourth tile to an existing exposed Pong during your turn.

After any Kong, draw a replacement tile from the dead wall. Winning on a Kong replacement tile earns bonus faan.

Robbing the Kong: When a player adds a tile to make an Added Kong, any other player may "rob" that tile to complete a winning hand. This also earns bonus faan.

Scoring (Faan Table)

Hong Kong Mahjong uses the faan (番) system to score hands. Each pattern or condition adds faan to your total. Payment is calculated as 2faan.

Payment

The winner's payment is calculated as 2faan base points.

  • Self-drawn win: Each of the three losers pays the winner equally.
  • Discard win: The player who discarded the winning tile pays the full amount.

Playing at the Sparrow

Getting Started

  1. Enter your name on the lobby page.
  2. Open a new table or join an existing one using a table code.
  3. When ready, the host clicks "Deal the Tiles" to begin.
  4. Empty seats are filled by house AI players automatically.

Controls

  • Click a tile to select it. Double-click to discard it immediately.
  • Select a tile, then press Space or Enter to discard.
  • When another player discards, action buttons appear if you can claim (Chow, Pong, Kong, or Win).
  • The drawn tile appears with a gap to the right of your hand.

Spectating

Join any table as a spectator to watch the game unfold. Spectators see all public information (discards, exposed melds, game log) but cannot see any player's concealed hand.

AI Players

Any seat not occupied by a human player is filled by a house AI. If a human player disconnects mid-game, the AI seamlessly takes over their seat. You can play a full game solo against three AI opponents, or with any mix of human and AI players.

Table Etiquette

  • Play at a reasonable pace. The table is for everyone.
  • Use the chat respectfully. We're all here to enjoy the game.
  • Study the tiles and learn the patterns. The best players see the whole table.
  • Remember: mahjong is a game of skill, patience, and a little bit of luck.
